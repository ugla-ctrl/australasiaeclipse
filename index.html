The event

One shadow. Two countries.
Five minutes of night at noon.

On 22 July 2028 the Moon’s umbra makes landfall on the Kimberley coast, crosses the entire Australian continent through the Red Centre and the heart of Sydney, then sweeps the South Island of New Zealand — all in under three hours.
